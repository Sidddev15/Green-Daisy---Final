How We Do Things?

Harvesting the Finest Tea Leaves

The first step is the plucking of the tea plant's harvest, which is called a flush. The first flush is the plucking of the first harvest in the springtime, while the second flush is the plucking of tea plants harvested after the first flush, typically in June.

Meticulous Processing for Natural Flavors and Aromas

Once the leaves have been picked, they undergo a meticulous processing procedure. The leaves are first withered to remove excess moisture, then rolled to release natural flavors and aromas, and allowed to ferment, giving the tea its distinctive taste and aroma.

Maintaining the Appropriate Temperature for Each Type of Tea

It is crucial to maintain the appropriate temperature during processing, which varies depending on the type of tea. For example, black tea is typically processed at higher temperatures than green tea.

Ensuring the Quality and Freshness of Our Tea Leaves

We at Green Daisy take great care to ensure that we use only the freshest and highest quality tea leaves, which are carefully processed to preserve their natural goodness. Our dedication to quality is reflected in every aspect of our brand, from our tea blends to our customer service.
